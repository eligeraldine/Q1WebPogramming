Historical City Of Surabaya

Sura and Baya

Surabaya is believed to have been founded some time towards the end of the thirteenth century. The name is a combination of the words for "shark" and "crocodile", and comes from a legendary battle between the two to establish which was the strongest animal. Statues and depictions of the battle litter the city.

10th November 1945

The events of 10 November 1945 were the battle in Surabaya which was one of the important moments in the history of Indonesia's struggle for independence. This battle took place between Indonesian troops and British troops, who at that time were trying to restore Dutch control after Japan surrendered.

This conflict was triggered by tensions between the British army and Indonesian freedom fighters, especially after the proclamation of independence on 17 August 1945. This battle was fierce and caused many casualties on both sides. Even though in the end the Indonesian troops were able to survive, this battle became a symbol of the spirit of the Indonesian people's struggle to maintain independence.

Every year, November 10 is celebrated as Heroes' Day in Indonesia to honor the services of the heroes who fought in the battle.

Majapahit Hotel

Hotel Yamato, which is now known as Hotel Majapahit, has an important history in the context of Indonesia's struggle for independence. This hotel is located in Surabaya and was built in 1910 by a Dutch businessman.

On 19 September 1945, this hotel was the location for a meeting between Indonesian leaders and British soldiers after the proclamation of independence. This is where the event known as the "Hotel Yamato Incident" occurred when freedom fighters tried to take over the hotel from the hands of the British army.

This meeting sparked tensions that led to armed conflict in Surabaya, which culminated in fierce fighting on November 10 1945. This incident illustrates the fighting spirit of the Indonesian people in defending independence and is part of the important history of Indonesia's struggle. The Majapahit Hotel is now recognized as a historical site and symbol of this struggle, as well as being an attractive tourist spot for visitors.
